# GM Score Guide — How to Climb the Leaderboard

> Your GM Score measures how well you evaluate talent, build rosters, and make trades as a general manager. This guide explains exactly how scoring works and what you can do to improve your rank.

---

## How Your GM Score Works

Your **Combined GM Score** is calculated from two activities:

| Activity | Weight | What It Measures |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Trade Simulator** | **60%** | Your average grade across accepted trades |
| **Mock Draft** | **40%** | Your best mock draft score |

- If you've only done trades, your Combined GM Score = your trade score
- If you've only done mock drafts, your Combined GM Score = your mock draft score
- If you've done both, it's **60% trade avg + 40% best mock draft**

Your goal: maximize both.

---

## Trade Simulator Scoring

### The Basics

Every trade you submit gets graded on a **0–100 scale**. Only **accepted trades (grade 70+)** count toward your leaderboard score.

| Grade | Label | What It Means |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| 90–100 | **ELITE** | Franchise-altering steal — extremely rare |
| 85–89 | **EXCELLENT** | Outstanding trade for Chicago |
| 70–84 | **ACCEPTED** | Good value — trade approved |
| 65–69 | **CLOSE** | Almost there — minor adjustment needed |
| 55–64 | **REJECTED** | Value concerns |
| 40–54 | **BAD** | Significant overpay |
| 20–39 | **TERRIBLE** | Do not make this trade |
| 0–19 | **ILLEGAL** | Untouchable player traded or illegal trade |

### What Counts on the Leaderboard

- **Only accepted trades (70+) add points.** Rejected trades add zero.
- Your **total score** = sum of all accepted trade grades
- Your **average grade** = total score / number of accepted trades
- Your **streak** tracks consecutive accepted trades (resets on any rejection)
- **Daily cap:** Maximum 5 scored trades per day during competitions
- **No duplicates:** Submitting the same trade twice won't double-count

### What the Leaderboard Tracks

| Stat | Description |
|------|------------|
| Total Score | Sum of all accepted trade grades |
| Trades Count | Number of accepted trades |
| Average Grade | Your consistency metric |
| Best Grade | Your single highest trade grade |
| Acceptance Rate | Accepted / total attempted |
| Streak | Consecutive accepted trades |

## What Improves Your Trade Grade

Trades are evaluated on six weighted criteria:

### 1. Value Balance (30% of grade)
The biggest factor. Both sides need to exchange comparable talent and contract value.

**Do this:**
- Match player caliber on both sides — star for star, role player for role player + sweetener
- Include draft picks to balance out gaps in player value
- Think about what the other GM gets out of it too

**Avoid:**
- Sending a star and getting back bench players
- Offering nothing of value to the other team

### 2. Realism (25% of grade)
Would the other GM actually accept this deal?

**Do this:**
- Make sure the other team has a reason to say yes
- Consider what the trade partner needs — don't just think about Chicago
- Study real trades for reference points

**Avoid:**
- One-sided trades where the other team gets nothing useful
- Trades that no real front office would consider

### 3. Team Needs (15% of grade)
Does this trade fill a real gap for Chicago?

**Do this:**
- Trade from positions of strength to fill positions of weakness
- Know each team's needs (see team-specific tips below)
- Acquiring a position you desperately need justifies paying a premium

**Avoid:**
- Trading for positions you're already stacked at
- Ignoring obvious roster holes

### 4. Contract/Cap Impact (15% of grade)
Smart salary management gets rewarded.

**Do this:**
- Create cap relief when possible — saving $5M+ earns bonus points (up to +18)
- Use salary retention in MLB trades to make deals more realistic
- Send surplus contracts, keep team-friendly deals

**Avoid:**
- Taking on massive contracts without getting star talent back
- Ignoring salary matching rules (especially in NBA)

### 5. Player Caliber (10% of grade)
Stats, trajectory, usage, and awards matter.

**Do this:**
- Trade declining veterans for ascending young players
- Value players on rookie/team-friendly contracts higher
- Consider recent performance, not just name recognition

### 6. Age & Future (5% of grade)
Youth and upside get a slight premium.

**Do this:**
- Target players under 27 (ascending value)
- Prioritize rookie-scale contracts
- Consider a player's trajectory, not just current stats

**Avoid:**
- Acquiring players over 32 without a clear win-now justification

## Cap Relief Bonus (Hidden Multiplier)

One of the best ways to boost your grade: **create cap savings.**

| Cap Savings | Base Bonus |
|------------|-----------|
| Under $5M | No bonus |
| $5M–$15M | +5 to +15 points |
| $15M+ | +15 points (capped) |

**Multipliers stack:**
- Contending team (Bears, Cubs) saving cap space: **1.25x bonus**
- Sending a player from a surplus position (not a need): **1.15x bonus**
- Maximum possible cap relief bonus: **+18 points**

**Example:** Bears trade a $12M WR (surplus position) and receive a $4M EDGE rusher. That's $8M saved. Base bonus = +8, contending multiplier = +10, surplus multiplier = +11. That's +11 points added to your grade just from smart cap management.

## What Tanks Your Trade Grade

### Instant Grade 0
- **Trading Caleb Williams** (Bears) — untouchable, non-negotiable
- **Trading Connor Bedard** (Blackhawks) — untouchable, non-negotiable
- **Blackhawks trade requiring salary retention when no slots are available**

### Grade 15–30 (Near-Untouchable Prospects)
Trading these without getting a superstar back:
- **Blackhawks:** Frank Nazar, Kevin Korchinski, Artyom Levshunov
- **Cubs:** Matt Shaw, Cade Horton, Moises Ballesteros

### Major Penalties
- **Trading a 1st round pick for a RB (NFL):** Grade 15–30. Running backs have almost no trade value.
- **Division rival trades (NFL):** -5 to -10 point penalty for trading with Packers, Vikings, or Lions
- **Acquiring expensive veterans for a rebuilding team:** Bulls, Blackhawks, and White Sox are rebuilding — expensive vets hurt your grade
- **Ignoring salary matching rules (NBA):** Over-the-cap teams must match within 125% + $100K
- **Violating the Stepien Rule (NBA):** Can't trade consecutive future first-round picks

## Mock Draft Scoring

Your **best mock draft score** counts toward 40% of your Combined GM Score. Key tips:

- Complete multiple mock drafts — only your best score counts
- If you've marked a "Best of Three" attempt, that score takes priority
- Completed drafts only — unfinished mocks don't count

## Leaderboard Tips & Strategy

### Maximize Your Score
1. **Quality over quantity.** Your average grade matters more than spamming trades. One 88-grade trade is worth more than three 72s for your average.
2. **Know your team's phase.** Rebuilding teams should sell veterans. Contending teams should buy talent. Trading with the team's strategy = instant boost.
3. **Use cap relief strategically.** The hidden bonus for saving $5M+ can push a borderline trade from rejected to accepted.
4. **Trade from surplus positions.** Sending players from positions of strength earns bonus points.
5. **Study real trades.** The grading AI references actual historical trades. Understanding real trade values helps you calibrate.
6. **Build streaks.** Consecutive accepted trades build your streak counter. Avoid submitting trades you know will get rejected.
7. **Read the suggested counter-trade.** When a trade is rejected, the AI suggests specific improvements. Use those suggestions — they're designed to grade 72+.
8. **Do mock drafts too.** They're 40% of your combined score. Even a decent mock draft score significantly boosts your overall GM rating.

### Common Mistakes
- Submitting lopsided trades hoping to "steal" players — the AI catches these
- Trading untouchable players or near-untouchable prospects for scraps
- Ignoring the other team's perspective (the trade must make sense for both sides)
- Spamming rejected trades instead of adjusting based on feedback
- Only doing trades and skipping mock drafts (leaving 40% of your score on the table)

### The 65–69 Zone
Trades graded 65–69 are labeled **CLOSE** — you were almost there. When you land here:
- Check the suggested counter-trade in the response
- Usually adding one draft pick or swapping one player tips it over 70
- A grade of 69 automatically rounds up to 70 (you won't get stuck at 69)
